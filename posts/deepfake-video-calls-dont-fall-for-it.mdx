<h3>How Deepfake Technology Works</h3>
<p>Deepfake technology utilizes advanced <strong>artificial intelligence</strong> models, specifically Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), to create hyper-realistic videos. GANs consist of two neural networks: the generator and the discriminator. The generator produces fake images or videos while the discriminator evaluates them against real samples; this back-and-forth process continues until the generator can create content that is indistinguishable from reality. As a result, you can witness your favorite celebrity or even a colleague engaging in conversations they never actually had.</p>
<p>It's the training of these networks on large datasets that elevates their effectiveness. For example, by feeding the AI countless images of a person's face, it can learn and replicate their movements, expressions, and vocal nuances. This means that in a deepfake video call, the synthesized image can mimic the subject so closely that it may even evoke emotional reactions from viewers, thereby increasing the potential for manipulation.</p>

<h3>The Algorithms Behind Deception</h3>
